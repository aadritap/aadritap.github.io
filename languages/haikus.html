By Santōka Taneda (種田 山頭火) (1882-1940):
Translations by R.H. Blythe

しとどに濡れてこれは道しるべの石
This is the stone,
Drenched with rain,
That marks the way.

鉄鉢の中へも霰
Into the iron bowl also,
Hailstones.


By Fukuda Chiyo-ni (福田 千代尼) (1703-1775, Edo period):

蜻蛉釣り今日はどこまで行ったやら
Dragonfly catcher,
How far have you gone today
In your wandering?

Her (the poet's) son passed away at a young age...
